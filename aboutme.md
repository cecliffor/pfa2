### Craig E. Clifford, DPM, MHA

With more than a decade of performing, teaching, and developing surgical techniques, Dr. Clifford recognized that most foot and ankle surgery can be avoided with appropriate conservative care. In 2022, he left his successful high volume surgical practice to focus on expanding patient access to cutting-edge nonsurgical treatment—though he continues to perform surgery when necessary, utilizing the latest advancements in minimally invasive techniques. Dr. Clifford understands that there is no "one size fits all" solution in medicine, and **every patient deserves an individualized, evidence-based approach, not a generic treatment plan**.

Dr. Clifford completed his podiatric medical and surgical residency training at the Franciscan Foot and Ankle Institute in Federal Way, Washington, where he later served as Director of Research and then Director of Residency Training. During his teaching tenure, he received multiple honors including **Clinician of the Year**, Instructor of the Year, and Outstanding Teaching Award. In 2020, he was named **Physician of the Year** by the Washington State Podiatric Medical Association.

Dr. Clifford is **Board Certified** by the American Board of Foot and Ankle Surgery and additionally is **Board Certified** by the American Board of Podiatric Medicine. He has previously served on the Board of Directors for the International Foot and Ankle Foundation and has held editorial and peer review roles for multiple medical journals. He was also actively involved for over a decade with the Yucatan Children's Project, where he volunteered his time and skills to perform deformity correction surgeries for underserved children in Mexico.
